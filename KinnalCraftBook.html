Kinnal Craft Book

96 pages to summarise a pivotal experience from 2174 photos, 22 pages of A4 script, lots of diary entries and heaps of funny stories and faint memories.

How often does one look at digital photo albums? I’ve been meaning to design this coffee table book for many years to properly summarise the crafts process, make use of the many artful photographs and to accompany the DVD and online video content I created just after the project's completion.

With it’s fifth year annoversary coming up some of the group members will be returning to the village of Kinnal to meet our old friends and to work alongside them once more doing Kinnal. A bunch of books are already packed waiting for their long journey.

My role

Along side with doing the layout and rewriting the voice over script from the documentaries, I also did illustrations to capture the finess of the craft. Ailsa Kay proof read and co-wrote. Preparing the file for lulu.com printing.

The Craft's process

Short documentaries summarising different aspects of living and working in an Indian village. These 6 documentaries were compiled in a DVD format and shared with sponsors and supporters.
