Kinnal Craft Book

96 pages to summarise a pivotal experience, the Kinnal Craft Project, from 2174 photos, 22 pages of A4 script, lots of diary entries and heaps of funny stories and faint memories.

How often does one look at digital photo albums? I’ve been meaning to design this coffee table book for many years to properly summarise the crafts process, make use of the many artful photographs and to accompany the DVD and online video content I created just after the project's completion.

With it’s fifth year annoversary coming up some of the group members will be returning to the village of Kinnal to meet our old friends and work alongside them once more doing Kinnal. A bunch of books are already packed waiting for their long journey.

My role

Along side with participating in the Kinnal Craft project, I collected and archived all photos, stories and media archicles from the trip. With improved layout and better English skills I ammeded the voice over scripts as text in the book, Ailsa proof read and co-wrote sections, and planned the structure of the book, preparing all illustrations and visual imagery. As this was a self initiated project I also prepared the file for print, ordered a proof reading copy and created new editions.

Printing experience with lulu.com

Previously I've printed and bound books myself e.g. the GetGo Glasgow book. For this project I tried out lulu's self publishing services as their size was the closest to the origal layout 210x210mm. Having suzzed the correct bleeding options with lulu customer service's help, convenience, price and speed of the service over-weighs the quality of the colour lazer print.
