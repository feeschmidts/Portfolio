Monchique Novas Direcoes

A three months Leonardo da Vinci programm to learn about sustainable tourism in Monchique, South Portugal. Collaborating with the council, residents and local businesses a small group of four responded to local opportunities and developed ideas to promote Monchique as an up and coming destination.

Monchique is situated at the foot of the tallest point of the Algarve, called Foia, amidst rolling hills and beautiful nature. It's the home to many German and English expats, alternative communities and sustainable minded guest houses who horne permaculture gardens, offer a low-tech lifestyle or just a place to relax. Annually three million tourists travel through Monchique to enjoy the view of the tallest point, leaving ten minutes later with local businesses in town missing out on the masses. There is more to Monchique than Fioa! and even the tourism office in town encourages tourists to leave and visit other places of the Algarve.

To address this five maps were created together with the council and local businesses which are easily downloadable and printed, suggesting itenaries of walks and visiting local craft makers. These with ideas to create a 'Monchique Brand' were showcased in a week-long exhibition called 'Monchique Novas Direcoes' (Monchique New Directions) with the intent to gain local's feedback, invite press and get Monchique noticed! The second room of the exhibition displayed all the process work and research as well as telling individual's experiences of the Leonardo Da Vinci programm for transparency and creating a better image.

Blogging & Documenting

On my Relvinhas Blog ideas and impressions and everything we did during the three months project are captured. My favourites are learning how to build a dry stone wall researching the tile making process of 'Alujezos de Portugal' and evaluating the seasonal Medronho experience.

For the exhibition Novas Direcoes I set up a seperate blog to capture the exhibition's content and be able to refer press and visitors to.

Internal Workshops

The purpose of the program was differently communicated to its participants, stakeholders and the village and lacked management. For the participants it was as broad as 'self development' with the broad aim to learn about sustainable tourism and work with local businesses. As far as the partnering local businesses were concerned, they gained free labour but did not see the advantages of having access to such an interdisciplinary group with a brought skill set. It required sharing goals and planning, ensuring everyone (participant and businesses) were doing something they enjoyed and would be beneficial long term.

Together with Julia I held interviews gaining insight into what everyone came to Monchique for and shared these with all stakeholders involved in a set of two workshops, planning activities for the weeks ahead together with the local coordinator. Details on this learning of group management can be read on the relvinhas blog.

Benchmarking & Research

Quinta brand development

During the summer the Quinta das Relvinhas offers a family all inclusive programm. During the winter however the facilities are more or less vacant with the occasional yoga, biking or walking group.

To explore the idea of turning it into a hostel during off peak, I conducted a hostel benchmarking research trip to Lisbon bringing back leaflets, pricing structures and interior ideas as suggestions for Frank, the owner of the quinta. Read the full analysis PDF.

What makes Monchique unique?

To understand Monchique's strenghts and opportunities we needed to benchmark Monchique against it's neighbouring villages. I went on a road trip to visit Salir's, Alte's & Silves' tourism offices and sampled their nature and village walks and the information they provided for guidance.

Independent tourism offices definately win to Algarve Tourism Board managed ones, like Monchique's tourism office.

Researching local crafts

Monchique has a union of local Medronho producers (the local fire water). Most craft makers are independent, working with some of the craft shops in town, which normally are familiy businesses as outlets producing chairs and ceramics to order from tourists who visited.
